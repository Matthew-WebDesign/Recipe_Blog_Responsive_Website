Loaded Baked Potato Soup

Loaded Baked Potato Soup
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ cups milk, or more, as needed
  • 3 russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste


A Chessy Appetizer


I never was one to eat soup before. It always seemed like the type of food to eat when you are feeling under the weather. But when I would go to Chilis with my parents my mom would often get the loaded baked potato soup for an appetizer. It smelled so nice and the cheese looked melted just right. It was exactly what I was looking for to replace the chips and salsa I would usually get for an appetizer. Now it's time to make the soup at home.


How to make the Loaded Baked Potato Soup

Step 1
Heat a large skillet over medium high heat. Add bacon and cook until brown and crispy, about 6-8 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate; set aside.
Step 2
Melt butter in a large stockpot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Whisk in flour until lightly browned, about 1 minute. Gradually whisk in milk, and cook, whisking constantly, until slightly thickened, about 1-2 minutes. Stir in potatoes and green onions.
Step 3
Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes. Stir in cheese, sour cream, salt and pepper, to taste. If the soup is too thick, add more milk as needed until desired consistency is reached.
Step 4
Serve immediately, garnished with green onion, cheese and bacon, if desired.
