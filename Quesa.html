Chicken Quesadilla

Chicken Quesadillas
Yields: 4 servings Prep Time: 10 mins Total Time: 40 mins Cal: 1020
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 1/2 onion
  • 1lb of boneless, skinless, chicken breast
  • 1/2 tablespoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1/2 tablespoon of dried oregeno
  • 4 meduim flour tortillas
  • 2 cups of shredded Monterey jack
  • 2 cups of shredded cheddar
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 scallions
  • Sour cream


A Sharable Entree


I only recently discovered chicken quesadillas after seeing my girlfriend get them (also) at Chilis everytime we went. I tried them out for the first time near the end of Summer and wow...just wow. It was amazing. I went on a quesadilla rampage afterwards. I got a variety of quesadillas from places like Explorers Den, Applebees, and Wawa. Trying so many varieties of quesadillas encouraged me to put it as the entre for my Recipe Blog. I want everyone who sees this blog to give this recipe a try and enjoy this amazing meal.


How to make the Chicken Quesadilla

Step 1
The first step to create a amazing Chicken Quesadilla is to sauté peppers and onions with salt and pepper You should cook them for around 5 minutes. so that there’s no unwanted crunch in the quesadillas. Once the veggies are an ideal texture, transfer them to a plate, and add in some vegetable oil to your pan.
Step 2
Add your strips of chicken breast into the pan, and season with your spices and salt and pepper to taste. Don’t go too far from your pan—you’re going to want to stir your chicken occasionally, and cook until golden and completely cooked through. Once the chicken is ready to go, transfer to a plate.
Step 3
Add your tortilla to the pan, then sprinkle on a very generous amount of both cheeses to half the tortilla, followed by your chicken, peppers and onions, a few slices of avocado, and sliced green onions. If you’re looking to add even more to your filling, now is the time to do it.
Step 4
Once all your filling is added, fold the other half of the tortilla over. Cook—flipping once—until perfectly golden, and any spilled cheese has turned into crispy, irresistible frico. (The real good stuff)Then you can slice them into wedges for easy eating.
